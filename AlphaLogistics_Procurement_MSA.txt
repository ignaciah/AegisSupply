MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT (MSA) - OPERATIONAL DISRUPTION & PENALTY POLICY
========================================================================
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Parties: AegisSupply Corp. (Buyer) and AlphaLogistics Components Inc. (Supplier)
SECTION 4: DELIVERY SCHEDULES AND DISRUPTIONS
4.1 Expected Delivery: Supplier must fulfill all Purchase Orders (POs) within the timeline
specified at the time of checkout.
4.2 Late Delivery Penalty Clauses:
- Grace Period: A standard grace period of 48 hours applies to all standard shipments from the
moment a "Delayed" alert is posted in the ERP tracking terminal.
- Disruption Level 1 (Minor Delay): If a shipment is delayed between 3 to 7 business days past
the expected delivery date, the Supplier agrees to issue an automatic 5% cost reduction penalty
on the affected PO line items.
- Disruption Level 2 (Major Delay): If a shipment is delayed greater than 7 business days, a 10%
penalty reduction applies, and AegisSupply reserves the right to source alternative parts from a
secondary marketplace vendor.
SECTION 5: ALTERNATIVE SUPPLIERS AND PRICE MATCHING
5.1 Sourcing Alternatives: In the event of a Disruption Level 2, the Buyer's autonomous
procurement agents are legally permitted to query third-party marketplace networks to find
instant-fulfillment replacement components.
5.2 Supplier Reimbursement: If alternative parts are sourced due to a Supplier fault, and the
third-party unit cost exceeds AlphaLogistics' contract price by less than $500, AlphaLogistics will
absorb the difference on the next monthly billing cycle invoice.
